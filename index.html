Air Plant Blooming Care

  • Some flowers bloom for a day, while others can last much longer up to a week or so.
  • Fertilizing is a good way to get your plant to bloom and produce pups.
  • Be careful when watering, not to submerge the bloom.
  • Once the bloom died, you can snip or remove to help encourage the next phase of growth.

Light

Air plants prefer indirect sunlight and should be placed where they will receive adequate light.

Water

Air plants need occasional misting 3-4 times a week or when they appear to be super dry. Soak for at least 10-15 minutes twice a month.

Soil

Air plants get nutrients in the air through their leaves. It is important to place them where they can enjoy a good air circulation.

Root System

Yes! Air plants has roots. It allows the air plant to grow even upside down.
